Business question

What makes this case interesting

The project combines data engineering and modeling. The data arrives at a daily episode level with missing values and duplicate rows, but the business questions are weekly and strategic: where to invest, how to measure channel impact, and how confident to be in optimization results.

I treated the work as an end-to-end product: clean the source data, define a Gold reporting layer, estimate response curves, simulate a policy change, and translate the model into a media budget decision.

What I shipped

Deliverables

  • A cleaned Gold weekly dataset built from Silver daily records
  • Campaign reporting tables with CPM and ROAS views
  • Three MMM variants: baseline, adstock, and adstock + saturation
  • A digital spend +20% scenario simulation
  • A constrained budget reallocation exercise tied to revenue

Workflow

How the analysis was structured

01

Audit the Silver data

Profiled the schema, checked lifecycle consistency by show and season, quantified missingness, and identified exact duplicates.

02

Build the Gold reporting layer

Imputed missing values within the same episode-week, removed duplicates, and aggregated the daily data into a weekly episode grain suitable for reporting and MMM.

03

Model channel impact

Compared a baseline log-spend OLS, an adstock model, and an adstock-plus-saturation MMM using per-show time-based splits.

04

Translate the model into decisions

Simulated a digital investment increase, then searched for a more profitable channel mix while keeping total budget fixed.

Model comparison

Why Model 2 won

Model Test R²
Baseline log-spend OLS 0.8956
Adstock MMM 0.9097
Adstock + saturation MMM 0.9135

The final model captured both carryover and diminishing returns, making it the most realistic specification of media response in this dataset.

Key interpretation

What the business should take away

  • Cable TV is the strongest driver of incremental viewership.
  • Network TV also performs well and improves the best budget mix.
  • Digital remains positive, but weaker at the margin in this case.
  • Lead-in bonus has a meaningful positive effect on outcomes.
  • Holiday is not a strong explanatory factor after controls.
